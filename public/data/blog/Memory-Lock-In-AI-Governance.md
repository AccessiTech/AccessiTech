# **Memory Lock-In: How Proprietary Harnesses Are Capturing Your AI Governance**

You've heard of vendor lock-in. You know it from databases, from cloud infrastructure, from SaaS contract cycles. Memory lock-in in AI governance is the same mechanism — but most procurement teams haven't caught up to it yet, and the window where the choice is still reversible is closing.

## **What a Harness Is**

The harness is not the model. It's the substrate that connects your model to memory, tools, and state — and it's where governance actually lives.

## **Why Governance Lives in the Harness**

If the harness controls memory and state, it controls behavior. Governance encoded at the harness layer is governance you control only as long as you control the harness.

## **The Proprietary Harness Problem**

LangGraph, LangChain, and similar orchestration stacks give you extraordinary capability — and concentrate ownership of your governance substrate in a vendor relationship you probably haven't stress-tested.

## **The Acquisition Scenario**

In early 2026, Meta acquired Moltbook, an AI agent orchestration platform. Within 48 hours, the Terms of Service changed. The liability that had been (at least partially) shared was now entirely yours — retroactively.

## **The AGENTS.md Open Standard**

Harrison Chase, CEO of LangChain, published a post in April 2026 arguing that harnesses are permanent infrastructure. An open-format governance substrate is the structural answer to proprietary lock-in — not a new idea, but newly validated for the AI governance context.
