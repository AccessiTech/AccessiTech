# Competitor Analysis: Content & Accessibility Metrics Summary

## Overview

This report reviews the content and accessibility metrics extracted from top-ranking competitor pages for your target keywords. The analysis covers word counts, heading structure, image/link usage, accessibility features, and basic readability, providing insights into current industry standards and opportunities for improvement.

---

## Key Findings

### Content Depth & Structure

**Word Count:**

- Most top-ranking pages have substantial content, with word counts ranging from ~1,000 to over 8,000 words.
- The average word count for high-ranking guides and checklists is between 2,000–5,000 words, indicating a preference for in-depth, comprehensive resources.

**Headings:**

- High-performing pages use 10–40+ headings, supporting clear structure and scannability.
- Some outliers (e.g., WCAG Plus) use over 100 headings, likely due to detailed breakdowns or long-form content.

**Images & Links:**

- Image counts vary widely (1–60+), but most guides include at least a few relevant images.
- Link counts are high (often 50–200+), reflecting extensive internal navigation and external references.

### Accessibility Features

**Images with Alt Text:**

- Most pages with images provide alt attributes, but the ratio of images with alt to total images varies. Some sites have all images properly described, while others miss alt text on a portion of images.

**ARIA & Semantic Markup:**

- Use of ARIA attributes and roles is present but inconsistent. Top accessibility-focused sites (e.g., w3.org, browserstack.com) use 10–150+ ARIA attributes per page, while others use very few or none.

**Form Elements:**

- Pages about web forms and accessibility best practices include more form elements, as expected.

### Readability

**Flesch-Kincaid Scores:**

- Most content falls in the 150–180 range (note: the scale here is not standard; typically, Flesch-Kincaid is 0–100, so this may be a raw or unscaled value).
- This suggests content is moderately complex, likely appropriate for a professional/technical audience.

---

## Insights & Recommendations

- **Comprehensive Content Wins:** Top-ranking pages are long, detailed, and well-structured. Aim for thorough coverage of your topics, using clear headings and sections.

- **Accessibility is a Differentiator:** Sites with strong accessibility features (alt text, ARIA, semantic HTML) are more likely to rank well for accessibility-related keywords. Ensure all images have descriptive alt text and use ARIA roles where appropriate.

- **Internal & External Linking:** High link counts suggest that both internal navigation and referencing authoritative sources are valued by users and search engines.

- **Visuals & Media:** Including relevant images (with alt text) and other media can enhance engagement and accessibility.

- **Readability:** While technical depth is important, strive for clarity and break up complex information with headings, lists, and examples.

## Next Steps

- Use these benchmarks to guide your own content creation and optimization.
- Consider a deeper qualitative review or AI-powered analysis for content quality, clarity, and accessibility best practices.
- Regularly audit your own pages for accessibility and SEO improvements.
