Use this template to document and prioritize keywords for AccessiTech’s SEO/content strategy.

## Instructions

- **Keyword / Topic:** The main keyword or topic phrase.
- **Search Volume:** Estimated monthly searches (from tool).
- **Difficulty / Competition:** How hard it is to rank (from tool or subjective).
- **Intent / Notes:** What users are looking for, or special context.
- **Priority:** High/Medium/Low (based on opportunity and fit).
- **Suggested Content Ideas:** Blog post, guide, checklist, review, etc.
- **Long-Tail Keywords:** More specific phrases (e.g., "best practices for accessible web design").
- **Question-Based Keywords:** Common questions users have (e.g., "What is the best color contrast for accessibility?").
- **Related Keywords:** Other relevant terms (e.g., "inclusive design", "web accessibility standards").

## Next Steps for Keyword Research

1. **Expand & Validate Your Keyword List**
   - ✅ Review for duplicates and overlap (e.g., combine similar keywords).
   - ✅ Add long-tail and question-based keywords (e.g., “how to make a website accessible for blind users”).

2. **Document & Prioritize**
   - ✅ Add new keywords and data (search volume, competition) to this template.
   - ✅ Mark high-priority keywords that are relevant and achievable.

3. **Analyze Competitors**
   - Google your target keywords and review top-ranking pages.
   - Note what topics they cover and what gaps you can fill.

4. **Integrate Keywords**
   - Use keywords naturally in your content, including titles, headings, and body text.
   - Avoid keyword stuffing; focus on readability and user experience.

5. **Plan Content**
   - Use your prioritized list to plan blog posts, guides, and resources.
   - Focus on actionable, evergreen, and in-depth content.
