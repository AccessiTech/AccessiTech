# WCAG Guideline Template Research Summary

This summary distills the findings from the automated comparison of your WCAG guideline markdown file and competitor page structures. It is intended to guide the creation of a flexible, best-practice template for all WCAG guideline explanations.

---

## Key Insights

- **Required Sections:** The following sections appear in most competitor pages and are recommended as core template elements:
  - Why it's important (user benefit)
  - What to check / How to implement
  - Common mistakes to avoid
  - Useful tools for testing
  - The four WCAG principles: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust
  - Solution / How to fix
  - Related posts / Further reading
  - Resources
  - Contact information
  - What is WCAG? (context/intro)

- **Optional Sections:** These are found in some competitor pages and can be included as needed for depth or context:
  - Accessibility checklist
  - Conclusion / Summary
  - FAQ
  - Table of contents
  - Company info
  - DART (Digital Accessibility Resources and Training)
  - Conformance levels
  - Introduction
  - Recent posts
  - Principle-specific breakdowns

- **Unique Sections:** Your current markdown includes unique sections (e.g., detailed explanations, checklists, or summaries) that can differentiate your content. Retain these where they add value.

---

## Example Template Structure

1. **Title & Metadata**
2. **Introduction / What is WCAG?**
3. **Why This Guideline Matters**
4. **How to Meet the Guideline (What to Check / How to Implement)**
5. **Common Mistakes to Avoid**
6. **Useful Tools for Testing**
7. **WCAG Principles (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust)**
8. **Solution / How to Fix**
9. **Resources & Further Reading**
10. **Related Posts / Links**
11. **Contact / Company Info**
12. **Optional: FAQ, Checklist, Table of Contents, DART, etc.**

---

## Recommendations

- Use the required sections as the backbone for all guideline pages.
- Add optional and unique sections as appropriate for the specific guideline or audience.
- Maintain flexibility to address unique aspects of each WCAG guideline.
- Regularly review competitor pages and update the template as best practices evolve.
